Artist Statement

A major goal of my work is to experiment with various forms that best convey a story or message. More specifically I am interested in the methods which structure the interpretation of a character's role in a given narrative. I enjoy visually constructing a model and imagining the ways the character might move and behave. Every visual style, from the drawings of Andrew Loomis to Adventure Time, inherits a unique tone which dictates what we expect out of a piece. Conceptually, I use a broader definition of characters and attempt to exploit our perceptions using the redefinition. By treating less traditional personifications as characters, such as social media profiles, I am interested in examining how we view characters against the burgeoning backdrop of Internet and the tools at our disposal to tell messages or stories in new and interesting ways. Currently, I have a particular interest in Internet Meme culture and the constructedness of net celebrities and personalities with their methods for retaining audience engagement. While modern Internet advertising practices (e.g intrusive video ads) focus more on increasingly modular and vapid ways to market new material, it's refreshing to see commitment to a newly established continuity. I aim to explore the "default state" of an online persona and the opportunities and limitations afforded to that persona. That is to say I enjoy analysing the state of a social media account before the user has established themselves into their account persona. Through future works, I hope to continue to investigate and redefine character development in a digital space.
